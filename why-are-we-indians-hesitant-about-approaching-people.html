Why are we Indians hesitant about approaching people?

Why so? Because we fear being judged by others. It scares us to even start a conversation. We feel anxious, scared and full of apprehensions while approaching people. Although we converse, there’s a constant consciousness that follows us around and we think twice before uttering anything. This happens because we don’t know the person. For us, approaching somebody can be daunting without finding the common traits that connect us.

Another reason why people don’t approach somebody is that they expect others to make the first move. Here, we take a back seat and wait for miracles to happen. It’s due to the shyness and reserved nature that we Indians inherently possess. Since our childhood, we've been told to behave appropriately everywhere we go. To be disciplined, to portray socially acceptable behaviour when we are in the public eye. So, we keep that at the back of our mind and maintain our boundaries.

If we observe American series, movies or even real-life situations, they always approach others first. If you’ve watched the famous sitcom ‘Friends’, you know how open they’re to getting acquainted with new people. For instance, if one of them liked a person romantically, they’d go over to them and ask them out on a date.

But in India, that’s not the case. Most people date via word of mouth. If you like somebody, you first tell your friend and then it indirectly reaches your crush. Here, few sources bring you together. We fear rejection, & so we overthink expressing how we feel. We’ve been conditioned with criticism rather than with praise. That’s why, we feel afraid to put ourselves out there.

The thought of failure stops us from discovering our true potential. If we aren’t self-confident, we won’t feel so while approaching others


How do we overcome this?
1. Don’t be scared to make mistakes

Though it’s a skill to talk to people, one can fail at it & that’s okay. Pick up those learnings and use it well next time.

2. Step out of your comfort zone:

Approach people. Practice. Eventually you gain confidence.
